Signature detail

Water goes where you cut it

Most washed-out property has the same story. Water found one low spot, dug a bowl, undercut the bank, and started taking ground a load at a time. Fixing it is not hauling in rock. It is cutting the section back to a shape water can travel through, then armoring the line it has to follow.

Pull the toggle to see the section we cut on a creek repair this summer: undercut bank scoured to a bowl beside a bridge, rebuilt as a benched channel with rock armor on the toe.
